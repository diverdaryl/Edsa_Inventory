Billboard Advertising in Metro Manila

Corridors, rates, formats, and live billboard inventory across the National Capital Region

The National Capital Region is the heart of Philippine OOH — over 13 million residents, millions of daily commuters, and the country's most valuable billboard corridors.

Why Advertise in Metro Manila?

Metro Manila concentrates the country's spending power, media attention, and daily traffic into just 620 square kilometers. Commuters here spend some of the longest travel times in Southeast Asia on the road — hours each day with billboards in clear view. For brand launches, FMCG campaigns, real estate, telco promos, and entertainment marketing, Metro Manila billboards remain the fastest way to build mass awareness in the Philippines.

Unlike digital ads that can be skipped or blocked, a well-placed billboard along EDSA or C5 is seen repeatedly by the same commuters every day, building the frequency that drives brand recall.

Key Billboard Corridors in Metro Manila

EDSA

The premier OOH corridor in the country, running 23.8 km from Caloocan to Pasay through Quezon City, Mandaluyong, and Makati. Landmark spectaculars near Guadalupe, Ortigas, and Cubao command the highest rates in the Philippines. Read our full EDSA billboard advertising guide.

C5 Road

The alternative north–south artery serving BGC, Eastwood, and Libis traffic. C5 has grown into a premium corridor as Bonifacio Global City matured — see our C5 billboard guide.

Commonwealth Avenue

The widest highway in the country (up to 18 lanes) moving huge volumes between Quezon City and Fairview. Strong for mass-market brands targeting northern Metro Manila households.

Quezon Avenue & España Boulevard

The main link between Quezon City and Manila proper, passing the Welcome Rotonda into the University Belt. España delivers heavy student and jeepney traffic toward Quiapo and Lacson.

Roxas Boulevard, Quiapo & Manila City Center

Dense pedestrian and PUV traffic with strong exposure for mass-market and remittance-driven categories.

Typical Rates in Metro Manila

Based on market estimates, static billboards in Metro Manila generally range from ₱80,000 to ₱300,000 per month, LED/digital placements from ₱150,000 to ₱600,000+, and premium EDSA spectaculars from ₱500,000 to ₱1.5M+. See the full breakdown in our billboard cost guide. Rates vary by traffic count, visibility, size, and lease terms — always confirm current pricing directly with operators.

Formats Available

  • Static spectaculars — large-format vinyl on steel structures, the classic EDSA format
  • LED/digital billboards — rotating spots, lower entry cost, flexible creative — compare in our LED vs static guide
  • Wallscapes and building wraps — high-impact placements on building faces in Makati, Ortigas, and BGC
  • Transit and street furniture — MRT/LRT stations, bus shelters, and PUV wraps to extend reach

Tips for Planning a Metro Manila Campaign

  1. Match corridor to audience. EDSA and C5 for broad reach and premium brands; Commonwealth and España for mass-market volume.
  2. Check both directions. Northbound and southbound faces of the same structure can have very different audiences and rates.
  3. Consider dwell time. Sites near chokepoints and intersections get longer viewing time than free-flowing stretches.
  4. Book early for Q4. Premium Metro Manila sites are often locked in months ahead of the holiday season.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a billboard cost in Metro Manila?

Static billboards in Metro Manila generally range from ₱80,000 to ₱300,000 per month, while LED and premium EDSA locations can run from ₱150,000 to over ₱1.5M monthly depending on location, size, and format.

What is the best billboard location in Metro Manila?

EDSA remains the most in-demand corridor due to its traffic volume, followed by C5, Commonwealth Avenue, and major intersections in Makati, Ortigas, and Quezon City. The best location depends on your target audience and budget.
