NLEX & SLEX Billboard Advertising

High-speed, high-impact large formats on the Philippines’ busiest expressways

NLEX and SLEX carry hundreds of thousands of vehicles daily between Metro Manila and Luzon's fastest-growing provinces — at highway speeds, with unobstructed sightlines built for large-format advertising.

Why Expressway Billboards?

Expressway advertising reaches a distinct and valuable audience: private car owners, logistics fleets, provincial commuters, and weekend travelers. Unlike city corridors where clutter competes for attention, expressway billboards stand alone against open sky, often visible from over half a kilometer away. For national brands, a NLEX or SLEX presence signals scale.

North Luzon Expressway (NLEX)

NLEX connects Metro Manila to Bulacan, Pampanga, and beyond — roughly 84 km of high-speed corridor. Key zones include:

  • Balintawak to Mindanao Avenue — the Metro Manila gateway, highest traffic density on the tollway
  • Valenzuela and Meycauayan — industrial and residential catchments
  • Marilao–Bocaue — near Philippine Arena events traffic
  • San Fernando / Angeles / Dau exits — Pampanga's commercial hubs and the Clark corridor — see also our North & Central Luzon guide

South Luzon Expressway (SLEX)

SLEX links Makati and the airport corridor to Laguna, Batangas, and the CALABARZON industrial belt:

  • Magallanes to Bicutan — dense city-exit traffic with premium sightlines
  • Sucat / Alabang — affluent southern Metro Manila catchment, near Filinvest and Ayala Alabang
  • Santa Rosa / Greenfield exits — auto industry hub, Nuvali and enterprise developments
  • Calamba and Batangas approaches — provincial gateway traffic and Batangas port logistics

Typical Rates and Formats

Expressway billboards skew large: 60-foot to 100-foot-plus static spectaculars dominate, with growing LED installations near toll plazas where slower speeds increase dwell time. Premium expressway locations are estimated at ₱500,000 to ₱1.5M+ monthly for landmark structures, while standard faces run considerably less — see our cost guide for full ranges. Rates depend heavily on distance from Metro Manila, structure size, and traffic counts.

Creative Rules of Thumb for Highway Speeds

  1. Seven words or fewer. At 100 km/h, viewers get 3–5 seconds of reading time.
  2. Large logos and single visuals. Detail disappears at distance; bold shapes win.
  3. High contrast. Structures face harsh backlighting at sunrise/sunset — test creative in both.
  4. Directional relevance. Northbound NLEX creative can target provincial audiences; southbound targets Metro-bound commuters.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a NLEX or SLEX billboard cost?

Standard expressway faces vary widely, while premium landmark structures on NLEX and SLEX are estimated at ₱500,000 to ₱1.5M+ per month. Rates depend on size, exact location, and traffic counts.

Are expressway billboards effective?

Yes — they reach private motorists, logistics fleets, and provincial travelers with long unobstructed sightlines and little competing clutter, making them ideal for national brand campaigns.

What size are expressway billboards?

Expressway structures are among the largest in the country, commonly 60 to 100+ feet wide, engineered for readability at highway speeds and long distances.
